# 1. STAFFING PHILOSOPHY & STRATEGY

## 1.1 THE "PARTY COMPOSITION" MODEL

Questpoint Cafe does not hire for a single "ideal candidate." We build **balanced teams** where different members bring different strengths. Like a well-built RPG party, we need variety.

**The Mistake to Avoid:**
Searching exclusively for "Barista Gamers"â€”people who are simultaneously:
- Excellent at hospitality and beverage preparation
- Deeply knowledgeable about Magic: The Gathering, board games, and gaming culture
- Available for cafe shift work
- In your geographic area

This unicorn exists, but not in sufficient numbers to staff an operation. Waiting for unicorns means understaffing, burnout, and compromised service.

**The Solution:**
Build teams where **gaming competency is distributed across shifts**, not required at maximum intensity from every individual. Every shift must have the collective capability to:
- Make excellent beverages
- Provide warm customer service
- Answer basic gaming questions
- Run scheduled events
- Handle equipment rentals

No single person needs to excel at ALL of these. The TEAM does.

# 2. THE STAFF ARCHETYPE SYSTEM

## 2.1 OVERVIEW

Questpoint uses a **four-tier archetype system** to classify staff capabilities and hiring needs. This is NOT a hierarchyâ€”it's a classification tool for building balanced teams.

| Tier | Archetype Name | Primary Strength | Secondary Strength |
|------|----------------|------------------|-------------------|
| 1 | The Barista Gamer | Both (Balanced) | Leadership potential |
| 2 | The Hospitality Pro | Beverage/Service | Learns gaming basics |
| 3 | The Gamer Convert | Gaming/Culture | Learns beverage skills |
| 4 | Remote Support | Specialized skills | Scalable across locations |

---

## 2.2 TIER 1: THE BARISTA GAMER

### Profile

**Who They Are:**
The unicorn. Someone who genuinely loves both excellent coffee AND gaming culture. They can discuss latte art and Commander deck-building with equal enthusiasm.

**Typical Background:**
- Former game store employee who learned coffee
- Barista who plays MTG/board games as a hobby
- College student who worked at Starbucks AND runs the gaming club
- Career pivoter from hospitality who discovered gaming

**Age Range:** 20-30 (typically)

**Personality Markers:**
- Naturally extroverted or "ambivert"
- Genuine enthusiasm that's visible (not performed)
- Takes pride in quality (both drinks and experiences)
- Problem-solver mentality
- Can read social situations

### Role in the Organization

**Primary Position:** Shift Lead (or GM track)

**Why They're Shift Leads:**
- They can handle ANY situation alone
- They're the "single point of coverage" for slow shifts
- They train and mentor Tier 2 and Tier 3 staff
- They're your emergency coverage when someone calls out

**Scheduling Priority:** 
Every shift should have at least ONE Tier 1 if possible. If not possible, pair Tier 2 + Tier 3 to cover gaps.

### How Many You Need

**Per Location:** 2-4 Tier 1 staff members
- 1 GM (if GM is Tier 1 caliber)
- 2-3 Shift Leads

**Reality Check:** These are hard to find. Budget 2-3 months of active recruiting before opening to identify them.

---

## 2.3 TIER 2: THE HOSPITALITY PRO

### Profile

**Who They Are:**
The "Starbucks Girl" (or guy). Excellent at customer service, beverage preparation, and handling rushes. Limited or no gaming knowledge initially, but **willing to learn the basics**.

**Typical Background:**
- Starbucks, Dutch Bros, Peet's, local coffee shops
- Restaurant servers, bartenders
- Hotel front desk, hospitality industry
- Retail customer service (Apple Store, Nordstrom-level)

**Age Range:** 18-35

**Personality Markers:**
- Service-oriented mindset ("How can I help?")
- Fast learner with new systems
- Comfortable with ambiguity (can figure things out)
- Friendly without being performative
- Doesn't look down on gaming culture (even if unfamiliar)

### Role in the Organization

**Primary Position:** Barista (Beverage-Focused)

**Their Superpower:**
During events (FNM, tournaments), 20-30 people are ordering drinks simultaneously. The Tier 2 staff member handles the rush while the gaming-focused staff runs the tournament. This is CRITICAL. A slow drink line during FNM kills the vibe.

**What They MUST Learn (The "Gaming Basics" Threshold):**
- How to check out/check in rental equipment (collateral system)
- How to explain "buy a drink, stay all day" to new customers
- How to direct customers to the right zone
- What FNM is and when it happens (basic awareness)
- How to say "Let me find someone who can help with that" gracefully

**What They Do NOT Need to Know:**
- MTG rules or card interactions
- Board game strategies
- Gaming meta or culture deep-dives
- How to run a tournament

### The Critical Requirement

**They Must Be Willing to Learn**

The difference between a good Tier 2 hire and a bad one:

| Good Tier 2 | Bad Tier 2 |
|-------------|------------|
| "I don't know much about Magic, but it seems interesting. I'd love to learn the basics." | "I'm just here to make coffee. The gaming stuff isn't really my thing." |
| Watches customers play with curiosity | Looks bored or dismissive during events |
| Asks questions to gaming staff | Avoids the back room entirely |
| Willing to try a board game during downtime | "I don't play games" |

**Interview Red Flag:** If a candidate expresses no interest in learning about the gaming side, they are not a fitâ€”even with excellent barista skills. The hybrid nature of the business requires baseline engagement.

### Scheduling Rules

**Never schedule two Tier 2 staff alone together.**

- Slow shift: Tier 2 can work alone (gaming activity is minimal)
- Medium shift: Tier 2 + Tier 1 or Tier 3
- Event shift: Tier 2 handles drinks, Tier 1/3 handles gaming

### How Many You Need

**Per Location:** 2-3 Tier 2 staff members
- Excellent for morning/afternoon coverage
- Critical for event night drink service

## 2.4 TIER 3: THE GAMER CONVERT

### Profile

**Who They Are:**
Your regulars. The customers who love Questpoint so much they want to work there. Deep gaming knowledge, embedded in your community, but may need hospitality training.

**Typical Background:**
- YOUR CUSTOMERS (the guy who's at every FNM)
- Local gaming community members (Discord, Reddit)
- Former game store employees
- Tournament grinders, content creators
- College gaming club members

**Age Range:** 18-35

**Personality Markers:**
- Genuinely passionate about games (it's obvious)
- Already has relationships with your regular customers
- Eager to help new players (not gatekeeping)
- May be introverted but becomes animated about games
- Shows up reliably (as a customerâ€”good sign for employment)

### Role in the Organization

**Primary Position:** Floor Staff (Gaming-Focused)

**Their Superpower:**
They ARE the culture. When a new customer walks in and asks "Is this a good place to learn Magic?", the Tier 3 staff member lights up and gives a genuine, enthusiastic answer. That authenticity is impossible to train.

**What They MUST Learn (The "Hospitality Basics" Threshold):**
- Espresso drink preparation (quality standards)
- Boba tea assembly
- POS system operation
- Food preparation (takoyaki, bao, pretzels)
- Opening and closing procedures
- Customer service scripts

**What They Already Know:**
- Gaming culture and norms
- Rules for major games (MTG, popular board games)
- How to teach games to new players
- Tournament organization and etiquette
- The community (they know the regulars by name)

### The Critical Requirement

**They Must Embrace Hospitality Standards**

The difference between a good Tier 3 hire and a bad one:

| Good Tier 3 | Bad Tier 3 |
|-------------|------------|
| "I know the games inside outâ€”teach me the coffee side!" | "I don't really care about the drinks, I just want to be around games." |
| Treats all customers warmly, even non-gamers | Only engages with "real gamers," dismissive of casuals |
| Willing to make drinks during rushes | "That's not why I'm here" |
| Maintains hygiene and appearance standards | Shows up looking like they just rolled out of bed |
| Can handle non-gaming customer questions | Gets annoyed by "basic" questions |

**Interview Red Flag:** If a candidate seems to want a job where they "just hang out and play games," they misunderstand the role. This is a SERVICE job that happens to be in a gaming environment.

### Scheduling Rules

**Pair Tier 3 with Tier 1 or Tier 2 until fully certified.**

- Tier 3 should NOT work alone until beverage certification is complete
- Ideal pairing: Tier 3 (gaming) + Tier 2 (drinks) covers all bases
- Event shifts: Tier 3 runs the tournament, others support

### How Many You Need

**Per Location:** 2-4 Tier 3 staff members
- Primary source of tournament organizers
- Best for weekend and event coverage

## 2.5 SHIFT COMPOSITION EXAMPLES

### Slow Shift (Weekday 10 AM - 2 PM)
**Staffing:** 1 person
**Ideal:** Tier 1 (can handle anything alone)
**Acceptable:** Tier 2 (gaming activity is minimal during these hours)

### Medium Shift (Weekday 2 PM - 6 PM)  
**Staffing:** 2 people
**Ideal:** Tier 1 + Tier 2 or Tier 3
**Acceptable:** Tier 2 + Tier 3 (if Tier 3 is beverage-certified)

### Event Shift (Friday Night Magic, 5 PM - 10 PM)
**Staffing:** 3 people minimum
**Required Composition:**
- 1 Tier 1 (Shift Lead, runs tournament)
- 1 Tier 2 (handles drink rush)
- 1 Tier 3 (assists floor, teaches new players)

### Weekend (Saturday 10 AM - 10 PM)
**Staffing:** 2-3 per shift, multiple shifts
**Morning:** Tier 1 or Tier 2 (cafe-focused crowd)
**Afternoon/Evening:** Full team if tournaments scheduled

# 4. REMOTE & CENTRALIZED SUPPORT ROLES

## 4.1 THE CASE FOR REMOTE SUPPORT

As Questpoint scales beyond one location, certain functions become more efficient when centralized. These roles:
- Don't require physical presence
- Benefit from specialized expertise
- Can serve multiple locations simultaneously
- Reduce per-location overhead

**Year 1 (Single Location):**
- Most remote tasks handled by GM or contracted out
- Budget: $200-500/month in freelance/contract work

**Year 2+ (Multi-Location):**
- Dedicated remote support staff becomes cost-effective
- One social media manager for 3 locations costs less than three part-time people

## 5.3 RECRUITING FROM YOUR OWN CUSTOMERS

The most effective recruitment pipeline is converting regulars into staff. Here's the process:

### Outreach Phase

**The Informal Conversation:**

*Timing:* During a slow moment, after they've been a regular for at least a month

*Script:*

> "Hey [Name], I wanted to mention something. We're starting to think about hiring, and your name came up. You're here a lot, you're always positive, and you seem to really get what we're doing here. Would you ever be interested in working at Questpoint?"

## 6.2 STAGE 1: APPLICATION

### Screening Questions (Critical)

These questions filter for culture fit before you invest interview time.

**Question 1: Gaming Engagement**

**What you're looking for:**
- Specific example (not generic)

- Resolution-focused
- Red flag: Can't think of an example
- Red flag: Blames the customer

- See candidate in real environment

| Criteria | Strong Signal | Weak Signal |

## 7.4 SCENARIO QUESTIONS (QUESTPOINT-SPECIFIC)

These questions test how candidates would handle situations unique to the gaming cafe environment.

### Question 8: The Gaming Knowledge Gap

> "Let's say you're working a shift and a customer asks you a question about Magic: The Gathering that you don't know the answer to. What do you do?"

**Ideal Answer:**
- "I'd be honest that I don't know, but I'd find someone who doesâ€”another staff member, or even ask if another customer who's playing could help."
- Shows resourcefulness and customer focus
- Doesn't fake knowledge

**Red Flag:**
- "I'd just tell them I don't know" (unhelpful)
- "That wouldn't happen because I know everything about Magic" (overconfidence)

### Question 9: The Hygiene Conversation

> "Part of maintaining our space is occasionally having difficult conversations. If a regular customer had a noticeable body odor issue that was affecting other customers, how would you handle it?"

**Ideal Answer:**
- Acknowledges the difficulty ("That's tough, but it's necessary for everyone's comfort")
- Private, respectful approach ("I'd pull them aside, not call them out publicly")
- Uses empathy ("Hey, I hate to bring this up, but...")
- Offers a solution ("If you want to step out and come back, no problem")

**Red Flag:**
- "I wouldn't say anything" (avoids conflict at expense of other customers)
- "I'd just tell them they smell" (no tact)

### Question 10: The Competitive Balance

> "During a Friday Night Magic tournament, someone accuses another player of cheating. Both players are upset. How would you handle it?"

**Ideal Answer:**
- Stay calm, don't escalate
- Separate if needed ("Let's all take a breath")
- Gather facts ("What happened?")
- Involve appropriate authority (Shift Lead, judge)
- Focus on fair resolution, not blame

**Good for Tier 1/3:** More detailed understanding of tournament dynamics
**Acceptable for Tier 2:** Basic de-escalation approach

### Question 11: The Buy-a-Drink Policy

> "A customer comes in and sits down at a gaming table without ordering anything. An hour later, they still haven't purchased anything. How do you handle it?"

**Ideal Answer:**
- Friendly approach first ("Hey, can I grab you anything?")
- Explain policy if needed ("Just so you know, we ask that everyone makes a purchase to use the gaming spaceâ€”it's how we keep the lights on")
- Offer options ("Our cheapest drink is $3 if you just want something simple")
- Enforce if necessary, but kindly

**Red Flag:**
- "I'd tell them to leave" (too aggressive)
- "I wouldn't say anything" (doesn't protect the business)

## 9.2 CERTIFICATION REQUIREMENTS

### Barista Test (All Staff)

**Format:** Practical demonstration

**Task:** Make 5 drinks selected by tester:
- 1 espresso-based drink (latte, cappuccino, or mocha)
- 1 boba tea (milk tea with pearls)
- 1 cold drink (iced coffee or cold brew)
- 2 of tester's choice from menu

**Scoring Criteria:**

| Criterion | Points |
|-----------|--------|
| Correct recipe and proportions | 5 |
| Proper technique (espresso extraction, milk steaming) | 5 |
| Speed (each drink under 3 minutes) | 5 |
| Presentation (clean cup, proper lid/straw) | 5 |
| **Total** | **20** |

**Passing Score:** 16/20 (80%)

### Floor Test - Basic (All Staff)

**Format:** Scenario-based evaluation

**Scenarios:**
1. Customer asks about the zones and what you do here (explain correctly)
2. Customer wants to rent a controller (process collateral correctly)
3. New customer asks if they can just sit and play (explain purchase policy)
4. Customer asks about upcoming events (provide accurate information)
5. Customer seems confused/lost (proactively offer assistance)

**Scoring Criteria:**

| Criterion | Points |
|-----------|--------|
| Accurate information | 5 |
| Friendly, helpful demeanor | 5 |
| Proactive service | 5 |
| Policy adherence | 5 |
| **Total** | **20** |

**Passing Score:** 16/20 (80%)

### Floor Test - Gaming (Tier 1 and Tier 3 Only)

**Format:** Practical demonstration

**Tasks:**
1. Teach a simple board game to the tester (as if they were a new customer)

3. Handle a mock rules dispute ("Customer A says X, Customer B says Y")
4. Describe the Commander format to a new player

**Scoring Criteria:**

| Criterion | Points |
|-----------|--------|
| Clear, understandable explanation | 5 |
