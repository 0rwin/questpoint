# 1. WHAT THE OPERATIONS MANUAL ACTUALLY IS

The Operations Manual is **THE product you're selling** for that $40k franchise fee. Without this, you have nothing. With it, you have a turnkey system someone can buy.

## The Test

Think of it like a **franchise Bible**. It's the complete instruction set for running Questpoint Cafe from Day 1 to Year 10.

If you handed this manual to someone who has NEVER worked in a cafe or run a business, could they:
- ✅ Open the doors on Day 1?
- ✅ Make every drink on the menu correctly?
- ✅ Handle a customer complaint?
- ✅ Run a Friday Night Magic tournament?
- ✅ Close out the register?
- ✅ Order inventory without running out or overstocking?

**If the answer is YES to all of those, you have a franchise-ready operations manual.**

If the answer is "well, they'd need to call me and ask..." then it's not done yet.

# 2. THE 12 MAJOR SECTIONS (COMPLETE BREAKDOWN)

## SECTION 1: FOUNDATION (The "Why")

### What You're Writing:
- Mission statement (copy from master doc: "Create a welcoming space...")
- Core values (Accessibility, Quality, Community, etc.)
- Brand personality (The "Companion" archetype - friendly, knowledgeable, reliable)
- Target customer profiles (The Core Gamer, Casual Socializer, Remote Worker)

### Why This Matters:
This section isn't operational - it's **cultural**. It ensures every franchisee understands what Questpoint IS and ISN'T. Prevents them from turning it into a dive bar or a stuffy corporate cafe.

### Time to Write: 
2-3 hours (this is the easiest section)

---

## SECTION 2: SITE SELECTION & BUILD-OUT (The "Where")

### What You're Writing:

**Chapter 2.1: Location Criteria**
- Demographics checklist (population, age, income)
- Proximity requirements (college campus, military base, anchors like Target/Starbucks)
- Traffic and visibility standards (10,000+ vehicles/day)
- Competition analysis (how close is too close to another game shop?)

**Chapter 2.2: Lease Negotiation**
- Rent budget (must be <15% of projected revenue)
- Lease length (5 years + 5-year option)
- Tenant improvement allowance ($10-20/sq ft from landlord)
- Red flags (avoid landlords who won't negotiate, dying plazas)

**Chapter 2.3: Build-Out Process**
- Timeline (12 weeks from lease signing to opening)
- Contractor selection (get 3 bids, check references)
- Permit checklist (health dept, fire marshal, occupancy)
- Equipment installation order (refrigeration first, POS last)

### Why This Matters:
Site selection is where most franchises fail. Bad location = guaranteed failure. This section protects the franchisee (and your royalty stream) from picking a terrible spot.

### Time to Write: 
4-6 hours (research lease terms, call commercial real estate agents for data)

---

## SECTION 3: LAYOUT & DESIGN STANDARDS (The "Look")

### What You're Writing:

**Chapter 3.1: The Mullet Layout**
- Floor plan template (with measurements - 600 sq ft cafe, 300 sq ft Zone A, etc.)
- Photos/diagrams (sketch the layout, even if it's rough - use Canva or draw.io)

**Chapter 3.2: Front of House Specs**
- Counter dimensions (8-10 ft long, 42" high)
- Menu board placement (above counter, backlit, 4x6 ft minimum)
- Seating arrangement (4-6 small tables, 2-3 medium, window counter)

**Chapter 3.3: Zone A (Analog Gaming)**
- Table specifications (48"x48" minimum, wipeable surface)
- Chair count (4-6 per table)
- Lighting requirements (4000K, bright overhead LEDs)

**Chapter 3.4: Zone B (Lounge/Lab)**
- Power outlet placement (every 4 ft along counter)
- Furniture specs (comfortable chairs for reading nook, task chairs for lab)

**Chapter 3.5: Zone C (Console Lounge)**
- TV mounting specs (wall-mounted, 55" minimum, eye-level when seated)
- Cable management (USB extensions, HDMI inputs, no visible wires)
- Seating (low couches, bean bags)

**Chapter 3.6: PC Gaming VIP Area**
- Locked room or gated section (secure when not in use)
- PC specs (RTX 4060, Ryzen 7, 16GB RAM minimum)
- Desk and chair ergonomics (adjustable height, cable management)
- Tournament setup (5-10 stations, LAN-connected, Discord/voice setup)

**Chapter 3.7: Signage & Branding**
- Logo usage (where it goes, minimum size, color variations)
- Exterior sign specs (backlit, visible from 100 ft)
- Interior signage (menu boards, zone labels, bathroom signs)

### Why This Matters:
Brand consistency. Every Questpoint Cafe should LOOK like a Questpoint Cafe. Customer walks into Norfolk or Virginia Beach and says "yep, this is the same vibe."

### Time to Write: 
6-8 hours (measure things if you have access to a space, or estimate based on research)

### What You're Writing:

**Chapter 4.1: Point of Sale (Square for Restaurants)**

- Menu programming (how to add drinks, modify prices, create combos)
- Employee management (clock in/out, permissions, sales tracking)
- End-of-day reports (how to run sales report, reconcile cash)

**Chapter 4.2: Inventory Management (TCGPlayer Pro)**

**Chapter 4.3: Scheduling (Homebase)**

**Chapter 4.4: Security Cameras**
- Camera placement diagram (over POS, trade-in counter, entry, storage)
- DVR setup (30-day recording, remote viewing via app)

**Chapter 4.5: WiFi Network**
- Guest network setup (password, bandwidth limits)
- Staff network (separate, for POS/back office only)
- Troubleshooting (router reset, Ethernet backup)

### Why This Matters:
Tech is where inexperienced franchisees get stuck. "How do I add a new drink to the menu?" "How do I pull camera footage?" This section gives them copy-paste instructions.

8-10 hours (you'll need to actually USE Square/Homebase to document the steps - sign up for free trials)

## SECTION 5: MENU & RECIPES (The "What")

### What You're Writing:

**Chapter 5.1: Beverage Menu**
- Complete menu with prices (copy from master doc, refine)

**Chapter 5.2: Recipe Cards** *(This is the most time-consuming part)*

Each drink gets a **recipe card** with:
- Ingredients (exact measurements - "12 oz milk, 2 oz espresso, 1 pump vanilla")
- Equipment needed (espresso machine, shaker, blender)
- Step-by-step instructions (numbered, with photos if possible)
- Quality standards (photo of finished product - "latte art should look like THIS")
- Common mistakes ("If foam is too thick, you over-steamed the milk")

**See Section 5 below for complete recipe card template**

**Chapter 5.3: Food Recipes**
- Same structure (takoyaki, bao, pretzels)
- Include: Prep instructions, cooking times, portion sizes

**Chapter 5.4: Themed Menu Rotation**
- Monthly schedule (January = League champs, February = MTG sets, etc.)
- How to create new themed drinks (naming, ingredients, presentation)
- Marketing rollout (announce on social media, limited-time pricing)

### Why This Matters:
Consistency. Customer orders "Black Lotus Latte" in Norfolk, it tastes the same as Virginia Beach. Recipe cards ensure this.

### Time to Write: 
20-30 hours (this is the LONGEST section - you'll need to test recipes at home)

## SECTION 6: STAFFING & TRAINING (The "Who")

### What You're Writing:

**Chapter 6.1: Org Chart**
- GM role (responsibilities, salary range, profit-sharing formula)
- Shift Lead role (open/close procedures, staff supervision)
- Barista role (drink-making, customer service)
- Floor Staff role (cleaning, equipment rental, event setup)

**Chapter 6.2: Job Descriptions**
- For each role: Duties, qualifications, hourly rate

**Chapter 6.3: Hiring Process**
- Where to post jobs (Indeed, Craigslist, college job boards)
- Interview questions ("Tell me about a time you handled a difficult customer")
- Background check process (optional, but recommended for cash handling)

**Chapter 6.4: Training Program**

**Week 1: Onboarding**
- Day 1: Orientation (mission, values, policies)
- Day 2-3: Barista training (espresso, boba, food prep)
- Day 4-5: Floor training (equipment rental, cleaning, events)

**Week 2: Shadowing**
- Work alongside experienced staff
- Practice making drinks (with supervision)
- Handle customer interactions (with backup)

**Week 3: Independent Work**
- Run shifts (shift lead present but hands-off)
- Get feedback, refine skills

**Week 4: Certification**
- Barista test (make 5 drinks to standard, timed)
- Floor test (handle equipment rental, run mock event)
- Sign-off by GM

**Chapter 6.5: Shift Checklists**
- Opening checklist (30 tasks, checkboxes)
- Mid-shift checklist (cleaning, restocking)
- Closing checklist (cash out, lock up, prep for tomorrow)

### Why This Matters:
Labor is your biggest expense (56% of revenue Year 1). Proper training reduces mistakes, turnover, and waste. Checklists prevent staff from forgetting critical tasks (like locking the door).

### Time to Write: 
10-12 hours

## SECTION 7: DAILY OPERATIONS (The "How - Every Day")

### What You're Writing:

This section is ALL checklists. Franchisees and staff should be able to open this section and see:

**"What do I do when I walk in the door at 9 AM?"**

**Opening Procedures (60 tasks):**
- [ ] Unlock front door
- [ ] Disable alarm (if applicable)
- [ ] Turn on lights (front, back, all zones)
- [ ] Start POS system
- [ ] Count cash drawer (verify $150 starting cash)
- [ ] Turn on espresso machine (15 min warm-up)
- [ ] Check coffee bean levels (restock if <2 lbs)
- [ ] Brew batch cold brew (if needed - 30-45 min)
- [ ] Cook tapioca pearls (45 min - start at 9:15 for 10 AM open)
- [ ] Check refrigerator temps (milk <40°F, walk-in <38°F)
- [ ] Wipe down all tables and chairs
- [ ] Refill condiment station (sugar, straws, napkins, toothpicks, chopsticks)
- [ ] Turn on music (Spotify playlist: "Questpoint Morning Vibes")
- [ ] Check bathroom (restock TP, soap, paper towels)
- [ ] Turn on "OPEN" sign
- [ ] Post "Good morning!" on Instagram

... (continue for all 60 tasks)

**Mid-Shift Procedures:**
- Check tables every 30-60 min (clear cups, wipe spills)
- Offer refills to customers
- Restock supplies (cups, lids, boba pearls)
- Monitor bathroom (check every 2 hours)

**Closing Procedures (50 tasks):**
- Lock front door at 9 PM (or posted close time)
- Clear and clean all tables
- Wipe down POS, counters, condiment station
- Clean espresso machine (backflush, wipe steam wand)
- Dispose of leftover boba (label with date if saving)
- Run end-of-day sales report
- Count cash drawer (compare to POS)
- Prepare deposit (cash + checks), lock in safe
- Take out trash, replace liners
- Sweep and mop floors (front and back)
- Turn off all equipment (espresso machine, TVs, consoles)
- Turn off lights (except security lights)
- Arm alarm (if applicable)
- Lock all doors, verify secure

### Why This Matters:
Eliminates guesswork. New staff doesn't need to remember 60 tasks - they just work down the checklist. Reduces errors, ensures consistency.

### Time to Write: 
6-8 hours (tedious but critical)

## SECTION 9: CUSTOMER SERVICE (The "Scripts")

### What You're Writing:

**Chapter 9.1: The Questpoint Way**
- Be Welcoming (greet every customer within 10 seconds)
- Be Helpful (offer assistance if customer looks confused)
- Be Knowledgeable (know menu, games, policies)
- Be Respectful (treat everyone with dignity)
- Be Proactive (check in every 60-90 min)

**Chapter 9.2: Service Scripts**

**First-Time Customer:**
"Welcome to Questpoint! Have you been here before?"
→ If no: "Awesome! Here's how it works: grab any drink, and you can hang out as long as you'd like. We've got gaming areas in the back - feel free to explore after you order. What can I get started for you?"

**Regular Customer:**
"Hey [Name]! The usual?"

**Equipment Rental:**
"Want to try a commander deck or grab a controller? I just need to hold your ID while you use it."

**Upselling (Natural):**
"Want to add boba for a dollar?" 
"We just got fresh takoyaki out - pairs great with that latte."

**Handling Complaints:**
- Step 1: Listen ("I'm really sorry to hear that. Can you tell me what happened?")
- Step 2: Empathize ("That's frustrating, I totally get it.")
- Step 3: Solve ("Here's what I can do: [refund, remake, discount next visit]")
- Step 4: Follow-up ("Does that work for you? I want to make sure you leave happy.")

**Chapter 9.3: The Bouncer Protocols** *(Hygiene & Behavior)*

**Hygiene Issue:**
"Hey, I hate to bring this up, but we ask that everyone maintains basic hygiene for the comfort of other customers. If you'd like, you can step out and come back after freshening up."

**Loud/Disruptive:**
"Hey, we love the energy, but can you keep it down a bit? Some folks are trying to focus."

**Cheating in Games:**
"We take fair play seriously. If you're not comfortable with the outcome, let's bring in a judge or take a break."

**Three Strikes:**
- 1st offense: Verbal warning
- 2nd offense: Written warning (logged in system)
- 3rd offense: 30-day ban (manager approval)

**Permanent Ban (Immediate):**
- Theft
- Violence
- Sexual harassment
- Illegal activity

### Why This Matters:
Scripts prevent staff from freezing up during difficult situations. "What do I say if someone complains?" → Open manual, read script, execute.

### Time to Write: 
4-6 hours

## SECTION 10: FINANCIAL MANAGEMENT (The "Money")

### What You're Writing:

**Chapter 10.1: Daily Cash Handling**
- Start of shift: Count drawer (should be $150)
- During shift: Max $150 in drawer (deposit excess in safe every 2-3 hours)
- End of shift: Run POS report, count cash, compare
- Discrepancy protocol (if cash ≠ POS, recount, notify manager if >$5 off)

**Chapter 10.2: Weekly P&L Review**
- Pull Square report (Sales, COGS, Labor %)
- Compare to budget (are we on track?)
- Adjust if needed (cut labor hours if revenue is low, increase marketing if traffic drops)

**Chapter 10.3: COGS Tracking**
- Calculate weekly (Total Food/Bev Cost ÷ Total Sales = COGS %)
- Target: 35% or lower
- If above 35%: Check for waste, theft, or over-portioning

**Chapter 10.4: Labor Cost Management**
- Target: 56% Year 1, <50% by Year 2
- Schedule efficiently (don't overstaff slow days)
- Avoid overtime (cap at 40 hrs/week per employee)

**Chapter 10.5: Monthly Budgeting**
- Fixed costs: Rent, insurance, software ($5,750/month)
- Variable costs: COGS, labor, supplies (scale with revenue)
- Net profit target: Break-even Year 2, 5-10% margin Year 3

**Chapter 10.6: Royalty Payment Process**
- Calculate royalty (Gross Revenue x 7%)
- Pay franchisor (you) by 10th of each month
- Submit sales report with payment (transparency)

### Why This Matters:
Franchisees aren't accountants. This section teaches them how to manage money without an MBA.

### Time to Write: 
6-8 hours

## SECTION 12: TROUBLESHOOTING & EMERGENCIES (The "Uh Oh")

### What You're Writing:

**Chapter 12.1: Equipment Failures**
- Espresso machine breaks → Call [Supplier], use backup (French press coffee until fixed)
- POS crashes → Restart, use offline mode, call Square support
- Refrigerator dies → Move perishables to backup fridge, call repair ASAP

**Chapter 12.2: Staffing Emergencies**
- No-show → Call backup list (maintain 2-3 on-call staff)
- Mass sick-out (flu season) → GM covers, reduce hours if needed

**Chapter 12.3: Health Inspection Failures**
- Minor violations (hand soap out) → Fix immediately, request re-inspection
- Major violations (temperature issues) → Close until fixed, notify franchisor (you)

**Chapter 12.4: Security Incidents**
- Theft → Review camera footage, file police report, notify franchisor
- Customer dispute → Separate parties, offer refunds, ban if necessary

### Why This Matters:
Murphy's Law: "Anything that can go wrong, will." This section prevents panic.

### Time to Write: 
4-6 hours

# 4. HOW TO ACTUALLY WRITE IT

## TOOLS

### 1. Google Docs (RECOMMENDED)
**Pros:**
- Free, cloud-based, accessible from anywhere
- Easy to share with franchisees
- Version control (track changes over time)
- Voice typing (speak instead of type - huge time saver)

**Cons:**
- Less professional formatting than Word
- Requires internet (though offline mode available)

**How to Set Up:**
1. Go to docs.google.com
2. Create new document: "Questpoint Cafe Operations Manual"
3. Create outline with all 12 sections as headers
4. Share with yourself (backup) and eventually franchisees

---

### 2. Microsoft Word (ALTERNATIVE)
**Pros:**
- Professional formatting
- Better for complex layouts (tables, diagrams)
- Industry standard for manuals

**Cons:**
- Requires license ($100/year or one-time purchase)
- Not cloud-based (unless using OneDrive)
- Harder to collaborate

---

### 3. Notion (ADVANCED)
**Pros:**
- Database-style organization
- Beautiful formatting
- Easy to link sections together

**Cons:**
- Steeper learning curve
- Less familiar to franchisees
- Harder to export as PDF

**Recommendation: Start with Google Docs. Simple, free, collaborative.**

---

## THE WRITING PROCESS

### Step 1: Create the Outline (Week 1)
- Use the 12 sections from this guide
- Create a Google Doc with headers for each section
- Don't write yet - just structure it
- Add placeholder text: "[TODO: Write opening procedures]"

**Example Outline Structure:**
```
QUESTPOINT CAFE OPERATIONS MANUAL

SECTION 1: FOUNDATION
  1.1 Mission Statement
  1.2 Core Values
  1.3 Brand Personality
  1.4 Target Customer Profiles

SECTION 2: SITE SELECTION & BUILD-OUT
  2.1 Location Criteria
  2.2 Lease Negotiation
  2.3 Build-Out Process
  
[etc.]
```

---

### Step 2: Write One Chapter Per Week (Weeks 2-52)

**The Sunday Writing Ritual:**
1. **Set a timer: 2 hours**
2. **Pick one chapter** (e.g., "Chapter 3.1: The Mullet Layout")
3. **Write 2-5 pages** (don't worry about perfection)
4. **Don't edit yet** - just GET IT DOWN
5. **Save and close** - you're done for the week

**Tips for Maintaining Momentum:**
- Same time every week (Sunday 2-4 PM while baby naps)
- Same location (kitchen table, home office, coffee shop)
- No distractions (phone on silent, close email)
- Reward yourself after (favorite show, beer, dessert)

**Voice Typing Hack (Game-Changer for You):**
- Google Docs has built-in voice typing (Tools → Voice typing)
- You can literally TALK the manual while doing dishes, driving (parked), or walking
- Clean up grammar later - just get ideas down
- 10x faster than typing with one hand while holding baby

---

### Step 3: Add Visuals (Ongoing)

**What You Need:**
- Floor plan diagrams (use draw.io or Canva)
- Photos of layouts (visit cafes, snap pics discreetly)
- Recipe photos (make drinks at home, photograph)
- Screenshots (POS system, scheduling software)

**When to Add Visuals:**
- During pop-up test (Year 2) - photograph EVERYTHING
- As you visit cafes (Year 2) - document layouts, menus
- During recipe testing (Year 2) - photo each drink

**Tools:**
- Canva (free): Create diagrams, floor plans
- draw.io (free): Technical drawings, flowcharts
- Smartphone camera: Quick photos, good enough quality

---

### Step 4: Test It (Year 2, During Pop-Up)

**The Real-World Test:**
- Use your manual to RUN the pop-up
- Did you forget anything? Add it.
- Were instructions unclear? Clarify.
- Did staff get confused? Simplify.

**Example Feedback Loop:**
- Pop-up staff: "How much ice goes in the boba tea?"
- You realize: Recipe doesn't specify ice amount
- Update manual: "Fill cup 3/4 with ice before adding tea"

**This is where the manual becomes GOLD.** You're not guessing what franchisees need - you KNOW because you've done it.

---

### Step 5: Professional Formatting (Year 3, Before Selling Franchise)

**What You Need:**
- Table of contents (auto-generated in Google Docs)
- Page numbers
- Headers/footers (Questpoint Cafe Operations Manual | Page X)

**DRINK NAME:** Black Lotus Latte

**CATEGORY:** Beverage | Coffee | Espresso-Based

**INGREDIENTS:**
- 2 oz espresso (double shot)
- 10 oz whole milk
- 1 oz activated charcoal syrup
- Pinch of edible gold dust (0.1g)

**EQUIPMENT NEEDED:**
- Espresso machine
- Steam wand
- 12 oz latte cup
- Small spoon (for gold dust)
- Thermometer (check milk temp)

**PREPARATION TIME:** 3-4 minutes

**INSTRUCTIONS:**
1. Pull double shot of espresso into 12 oz cup
2. Steam milk to 150-160°F (microfoam texture - velvety, not bubbly)
3. Add 1 oz charcoal syrup to espresso, stir with spoon
4. Pour steamed milk slowly into cup (aim for center, create latte art if trained)
5. Sprinkle pinch of gold dust on top of foam
6. Serve immediately

**QUALITY CHECK:**
- **Appearance:** Deep black liquid with white microfoam, gold shimmer on top, latte art visible (if created)
- **Temperature:** 140-150°F when served (hot but not scalding)
- **Taste:** Rich espresso flavor with subtle activated charcoal earthiness, smooth milk, not bitter

**COMMON MISTAKES:**
- **Over-steaming milk** (too hot, >170°F) → Burns flavor, makes it taste flat. Solution: Watch thermometer, stop at 160°F.
- **Too much charcoal syrup** (>1.5 oz) → Bitter, medicinal taste. Solution: Measure precisely, don't eyeball.
- **Lumpy gold dust** (clumps on top) → Unprofessional look. Solution: Use fine pinch, distribute evenly with gentle shake.
- **Milk not frothed** (steamed but no foam) → Flat latte, no latte art. Solution: Position steam wand just below surface, create vortex.

**ALLERGENS:** Dairy

**DIETARY NOTES:** 
- Dairy-free: Substitute oat milk or almond milk (note: oat milk froths better)
- Vegan: Use oat milk (already vegan, no other changes needed)
- Decaf: Use decaf espresso (no other changes)

**COST BREAKDOWN:**
- Espresso (2 oz): $0.40
- Whole milk (10 oz): $0.60
- Charcoal syrup (1 oz): $0.30
- Gold dust (pinch): $0.10
- Cup + lid: $0.25
- **TOTAL COST:** $1.65
- **SELL PRICE:** $6.50
- **GROSS PROFIT:** $4.85 (75% margin)
